Discover the Jewel of Micronesia: Where Nature meets Tradition

Nestled in the heart of the Pacific Ocean, Pohnpei is a captivating tropical paradise that beckons travelers with its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. As the largest and most populous of the Federated States of Micronesia, Pohnpei boasts lush rainforests, vibrant coral reefs, and stunning waterfalls, creating an idyllic backdrop for unforgettable adventures.

What To See In Pohnpei

Kepirohi Waterfalls is a breathtaking cascade hidden within Pohnpei's lush rainforest, offering a tranquil oasis for nature enthusiasts and explorers.

Sokehs Rock is a majestic natural landmark rising from the sea near Pohnpei, a symbol of the island's rugged beauty and cultural significance.

Nan Madol is an astonishing ancient city built upon a series of islets in the waters of Pohnpei, characterized by its intricate stone architecture and enigmatic history.

The Pohnpei Parrot, also known as the Pohnpei Lorikeet, is a vibrant and endangered bird species native to Pohnpei, characterized by its colorful plumage and unique presence in the island's ecosystem.
